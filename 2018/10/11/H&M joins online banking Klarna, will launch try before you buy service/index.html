H&M joins online banking Klarna, will launch try before you buy service

News Financial 2018/10/11

H&M, the world’s second largest clothing company, bought less than 1% of Swedish financial technology company Klarna. The two did not disclose the specific amount, the Financial Times reported that the transaction amount was around 189 million Swedish kronor (about 20 million US dollars).

H&M’s move is to connect online and offline shopping, enhance the payment experience, and streamline its return and exchange process. The scope of cooperation will also include the membership system. The partnership will begin in Sweden in 2019, covering 14 countries including Europe, including the UK, and then expanding to the US and Asia.

Klarna’s CEO Sebastian Siemiatkowski said the H&M shareholding was an “important deal.”

Klarna was founded in 2005 with the latest valuation (2015) at around $2.5 billion. It has a large market share in Germany and Northern Europe; 40% of online transactions in Sweden are carried out through Klarna. About 60 million consumers have used Klarna online shopping. In 2017, it obtained a banking license in Sweden.

Visa holds less than 10% of the company’s shares. The Danish brand Vera Moda, the parent company of Jack & Jones, Bestseller has over 10% of Klarna. Private equity firm Permira is also a shareholder of Klarna.

According to Klarna CEO Sebastian Siemiatkowski, H&M is the company’s third largest customer after the online fast fashion Asos and IKEA. It’s worth mentioning that Bestseller is the biggest shareholder of Asos, so it’s not surprising that the latter uses Klarna’s services.

Klarna also has a feature of first receiving and paying. Daniel Claesson, director of business development at H&M, said the partnership may also include this feature, allowing consumers to pay for the goods after they have been tried. Both Asos and Topshop have enabled this feature in the UK. After third-party credit agencies review consumers, they can use Klarna to “try” these merchants and make payments within thirty days. The Financial Times believes that this is just another form of packaging for consumer loans.

In any case, H&M took another step in the online business. It was one of the first online fashion brands to start online: it began experimenting with online retailing in 1998, but it has since neglected this channel and expanded rapidly through offline stores.

H&M in the Chinese market is a microcosm. Since 2012, the average annual net increase in stores has been 71. But in this country that loves online shopping, H&M knows about e-commerce, and it only launched official e-commerce in 2014, and it was not until March 2018 that it officially entered Tmall. The financial report mentioned that the Tmall store opened in the first quarter contributed a lot to the sales in the second quarter of China, but did not disclose the specific value.

H&M currently has physical stores in 67 countries and regions, of which 47 have opened online businesses, including the latest in India and the United Arab Emirates. The latest quarterly report did not mention the online penetration rate of the entire company, only showing that online business grew by 17% year-on-year. At the end of last year, H&M officially gave the data: 12.5% ​​of sales came from online.
In the second quarter of 2018, H&M sales increased 2% year-on-year to SEK 60.5 billion, but pre-tax profit fell 22% to SEK 6 billion, close to analysts’ expectations.
