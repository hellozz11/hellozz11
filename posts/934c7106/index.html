Why do people accept hurt, anger, despicableness, jealousy, hatred, evil desires, and shameful greed

knowledge 2018/10/11

Accepting bad is to make yourself better.

This sentence is not chicken soup, it is one of the conclusions of psychology about “self” research. Self, that is, “Who am I?”, this issue has aroused the interest of many people, such as “I only know that I know nothing” Socrates, “I think so I am” Descartes, and Jackie Chan and Huang Hey. In psychology research, “self-concept” refers to a person’s understanding of his personality traits, behaviors, and other personal characteristics. When a person describes himself, what is expressed is the most basic “self-concept.”

Why is “self-concept” so concerned? Because we don’t know what kind of person we are, we will be confused when we face things big and small in our lives. We don’t know how to choose. Once this understanding is stabilized, it will affect everything we have, affecting our concerns, memories, emotions, behaviors and even health.

People’s life is looking for answers for “who am I.” From childhood ignorance to the germination of adolescence to “Thirty, forty, no confusion”, from the parents’ shackles to the school’s education and friends’ evaluations, especially when facing the self in the mirror or the nightmare Self-evaluation in difficult times, these experiences help us shape the “self-concept”.

The ideal “self-concept” should be real and comprehensive, and it is difficult to do this. Most people tend to look at themselves positively, which is commonly referred to as “self-feeling good”, but some people only see their positive side, do not recognize their negative side, and move toward “self-centered” or “extreme self-esteem”. Love”; some people only see their negative side, feel that they are nothing, extremely inferiority, these people are often more likely to suffer from depression, anxiety and other mental disorders.

In other words, in order to create a “self-concept” that gives us a sense of security and identity, we should know ourselves and evaluate ourselves from multiple perspectives. We must have advantages, and we must have shortcomings. The significance of the shortcoming is to remind us that we can do better and do better.

As the title says, if these bad things are their own “one side,” accepting these bad things is a prerequisite for the formation of a correct “self-concept.” What’s more, these so-called “bad side” are not necessarily bad. Fear and anger are important to the evolution of human beings and are the key signals for us to avoid danger.
