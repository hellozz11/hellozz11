Americans are increasingly inclined to think that Trump will be re-elected

News 2018/10/16

According to a recent survey conducted by the US SSRS polling company for CNN, Americans are increasingly inclined to believe that President Donald Trump will win a second presidential term, while the former vice president Biden’s support rate is ahead of a group of Democrats who might want to replace Trump.

According to a report on the US Cable News website on October 14, the public disagreed over whether they thought Trump would win a second term—46% claimed he would be re-elected and 47% thought he would not be re-elected. However, for Trump, this is a huge improvement since March, when 54% of adults said they thought that Trump’s efforts to pursue a second term would end in failure. The proportion of the two parties who think that Trump will win the election again has increased. Compared with other groups, the proportion is more male (up 8 percentage points), independents (from 39% in March to 47% now), and people who are keen to participate in this year’s congressional elections (from 3 37% of the month rose to 46% today).

According to the report, among Trump’s staunch supporters, the proportion of people who want him to be nominated again for the Republican presidential nomination is the same as in March: 74% of Republicans and Republican-oriented people say that Trump should get the The party’s 2020 presidential nomination is nominated, and another 21% expect others to stand out among the party’s candidates.

The report also said that in the battle for a Democratic presidential nomination (in a confrontation with Trump), former US Vice President Biden may lead significantly. The above survey requires Democrats and independents with a democratic tendency to select their preferred presidential nominees from among the 16 possible Democratic candidates. Biden topped the list with 33% support, and the independent, Vermont federal Senator Bernie Sanders followed with a 13% approval rate. This was followed by California State Senator Kamala Harris with a 9% support rate and Massachusetts Senator Elizabeth Warren with a 8% support. New Jersey Senator Corey Booker and the 2004 Democratic presidential nominee John Kerry received a 5% approval rate.

In the survey, the former New York City Mayor Michael Bloomberg, who recently registered as a Democrat, has a 4% approval rate.

According to the report, the survey was conducted by the SSRS polling company between October 4 and 7. The investigators used a fixed number of telephones or mobile phones to collect a random sample of people from across the country (including 1009 adults). )We conducted a survey. The sampling error of the whole sample is plus or minus 3.8 percentage points.
