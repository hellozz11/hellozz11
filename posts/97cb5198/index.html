The United States has increased its overseas investment review of the United States, involving 27 industries

News 2018/10/11

The US Treasury Department announced on October 10 the details of foreign investment in the US based on the new bill passed in August. Twenty-seven industries, including semiconductors, information and communications, and military, were designated as restricted targets, and small-scale capital contributions were required to be reported to the US government in advance, and implemented on November 10. The US hopes to avoid cutting-edge technology into China.

US President Trump signed a new bill in mid-August to strengthen the corporate review of the US Foreign Investment Review Board (CFIUS). If CFIUS believes that foreign investment in the United States threatens US security, it is the responsibility of the President to stop the investment.

For the industry that has become a restricted target, the US Treasury Department requires foreign-funded enterprises to submit a declaration to CFIUS at least 45 days before the completion of the investment. If it is violated, it will be fined up to the same amount as the scheduled transaction amount. Previously, CFIUS took the foreign investment in US companies as the main subject of review, and did not require the company to make an advance declaration.

The areas to be restricted include aviation engines and parts, aluminum smelting, petrochemicals, etc., and are specified in detail. In addition, the development of nanotechnology and the manufacture of optical lenses are also subject to restrictions.

The U.S. Treasury Department explained to the review object that “there is no specific country,” but it is obviously aimed at China. In January, the financial company of Alibaba Group failed to obtain CFIUS approval and abandoned the acquisition of a large US remittance service company.

The Trump regime is strengthening its stance against China from a trade and military perspective. The United States believes that it is necessary to maintain international competitiveness by protecting the high technology of US companies while preventing China from transferring related technologies to the military industry.
