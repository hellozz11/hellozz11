The 2018 US National Book Awards finalists were released, and the first place in translation literature was won

Reader News 2018/10/11

On October 10th, the 2018 US National Book Awards shortlist was announced. 25 This bibliography is from the 1637 book and explores some of the most contemporary topics such as racism, masculinity, drug addiction, class division and civil rights.

This year, the National Book Awards set a separate category for translation literature. This is the first time since 1983 that the National Book Foundation has added new categories and is the first to recognize translations of foreign language literature.

“The less we know about the outside world, the worse we are,” said Lisa Lucas, executive director of the National Book Foundation. The New York Times reported that the Foundation’s board of directors hopes to draw attention to translations, which are often overlooked by American readers and publishers.

This also means that the National Book Award, which recognizes the best American literature, opened its doors to non-English writers at the age of 69.

The shortlist for translation literature includes writers from Iran, Norway, Italy, Japan and Poland.

Julpa Lahiri, a Pulitzer Prize-winning author and literary award frequentor, appeared on the list as a translator. She translated the novel by the Italian novelist Domenico Starnone, “The Trick” (“Trick “). This book tells the story of an old illustrator and a grandson who explores the complex relationship between growth and aging.

On November 13th, the authors of the finalists will read the excerpts of the work at the New College of New York City. The winners will be announced at the awards ceremony the following day.

Each of the award-winning authors will receive a prize of US$10,000, in addition to the Translation Literature Award, which will be awarded to the author and translator in two parts.
