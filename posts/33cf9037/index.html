Rozil rejects the Green Army’s 12 million annual salary. He will seek a big contract in the summer

News 2018/10/16

A report from ESPN’s famous journalist Adrian Wernalovsky said that informed sources said that the Celtics and defender Terry Rozil would not reach an early contract extension. The reason is that the two sides have a big gap in the contract price.

Monday afternoon local time is the 2015 renewal of the rookie early renewal deadline. According to the source, Rozier and the Green Army had a friendly conversation and the two sides will meet again in the summer. It is reported that Rozil will become a restricted free agent in the summer of 2019, when the Green Army can match the quotation contract issued by any team.

It was reported that the Green Army provided Rozil with a $12 million annual salary contract but was rejected. According to previous rumors, Rozil hopes to find a starting point guard level contract and playing time in the summer, he expected an annual salary of up to 20 million US dollars.

In an interview, Rozil said: “I am in a good position now. My team thinks that I don’t have to renew my contract in advance, which gives me more choices in the future.”

It can be seen that Rozil and his team believe that Rozil will have a chance to get a big contract in the summer.

The 18-19 season is the last year of Rozil’s rookie contract, with an annual salary of $3 million. In the offseason, Kyrie Irving announced that he will stay in Boston in the summer. This is a bit embarrassing for Rozil. He will return to the bench to lead the second team.

Owen’s early reimbursement for a knee injury last season gave Rozil a good chance. During the playoffs, Rozil played 36.6 minutes per game, surrendering 16.5 points, 5.7 assists, 5.3 rebounds and 1.3 steals, contributing to the Greens eventually reaching the Eastern Conference finals.

The 24-year-old Rozil has been in the league for three years. Last season, he played 80 regular season games for the Green Army, including 16 starts, averaging 25.9 minutes, 11.3 points, 4.7 rebounds, 2.9 assists and 1.0 steals.
